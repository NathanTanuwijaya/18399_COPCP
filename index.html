  IDENTIFYING CRITICAL SYSTEM

Good System Need

  • Reliability:Reliable systems ensure smooth business operations without unexpected downtimes.
  • Scalability:scalable system can accommodate business growth without needing constant upgrades.
  • User-Friendliness:User-friendly systems reduce training time and errors, improving overall efficiency.
  • Integration Capability:Good integration capabilities ensure seamless data flow and operational harmony across different platforms.
  • Security: A secure system protects against data breaches and ensures compliance with privacy regulations.

If System Bad

  • Weak Security:Weak security can lead to data breaches, legal issues, and loss of customer trust.
  • Poor User Experience:A complicated system increases training costs, user frustration, and operational errors
  • Unreliability: Frequent failures disrupt business operations and lead to loss of productivity

    • System Need Support

    • Technical Support:24/7 technical support ensures issues can be resolved quickly, minimizing downtime.
    • Maintenance and Updates:Consistent maintenance and timely updates help prevent technical issues and enhance system performance.
    • Documentation and Resources:Access to detailed documentation helps users troubleshoot issues independently and understand system functionalities.

      • Who will user System

      • Employees:Employees who depend on the system for their work need it to be reliable and efficient to maintain productivity.
      • Customers:Customer-facing systems need to be user-friendly and reliable to ensure a positive customer experience.
      • IT Support Staff:IT staff need robust tools and access to system diagnostics to maintain system health and address issues promptly.
